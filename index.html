Publications & Patents

Linear Push Policies to Increase Grasp Access for Robot Bin Picking
M. Danielczuk, J. Mahler, C. Correa, K. Goldberg  ·  IEEE CASE 2018, Munich, Germany

When a robot tries to grasp items from a cluttered bin, many objects aren't reachable in their current position — blocked, lying flat, or buried. This paper developed and benchmarked linear push motions that rearrange bin contents to expose better grasping surfaces, using the DexNet grasp-quality network to score candidate pushes. Validated in simulation and on a physical robot.

78 citations Conference
Robust Toppling for Vacuum Suction Grasping
C. Correa, J. Mahler, M. Danielczuk, K. Goldberg  ·  IEEE CASE 2019, Vancouver, BC, Canada

Suction-cup robots often encounter objects with no accessible top surface — a lying-flat bottle, a face-down part. Rather than skipping them, a robot can deliberately topple the object onto a better side. This paper derives a physics model predicting how a rigid object lands after being pushed, so the robot can plan a single contact that reliably exposes a graspable surface. The model was integrated with DexNet to jointly optimize the push and subsequent grasp.

18 citations Conference
A Cloud Robot System Using the Dexterity Network and Berkeley Robotics and Automation as a Service (BRASS)
N. Tian, M. Matl, J. Mahler, Y. Zhou, S. Staszak, C. Correa, et al.  ·  IEEE ICRA 2017

Demonstrated a cloud-based robotics system where lightweight robot clients offload expensive grasp planning to a remote server running the Dexterity Network (DexNet). By moving perception and planning to the cloud, robots can access large-scale GPU compute without carrying it onboard — enabling more capable and economical deployed systems at scale.

50 citations Conference
Computer-Automated Robot Grasp Depth Estimation
B. Goodrich, A. Kuefler, W. Richards, C. Correa, R. Sharma, S. Kumra  ·  US Patent 12,236,340  ·  USPTO, 2025

Determining how deep a robot's suction cup must reach to reliably grasp an item is non-trivial: too shallow and the seal fails, too deep and you collide with neighboring objects. This patent covers a computer-vision pipeline that automatically estimates the correct grasp depth from camera images, enabling a robot system to handle a wide variety of item sizes and shapes without manual calibration per SKU.
